Ulu Cami (Grand Mosque)

Nestled in the historic heart of Bursa, Ulu Cami (Grand Mosque) is not just a religious site—it is a living testament to the early soul of the Ottoman Empire. As the largest mosque in Bursa and one of the most significant in Turkey, Ulu Cami represents a blend of architectural grandeur, spiritual devotion, and deep-rooted cultural heritage that captivates every traveler who steps inside.

A Gift from a Sultan

Commissioned by Sultan Bayezid I in the late 14th century after his victory at the Battle of Nicopolis, Ulu Cami was built between 1396 and 1399. Legend has it that the sultan had vowed to build 20 mosques, but his architects suggested constructing one grand mosque with 20 domes, honoring his promise in a majestic and unified form. This clever solution gave rise to a unique architectural feature not commonly seen in other mosques of the period.
