Things to Do in Bursa

Explore, experience, enjoy!

Discover the Best Activities in Bursa!

From majestic mountains to historic mosques and vibrant bazaars, Bursa offers unforgettable experiences for every traveler.

Trilye

Nestled along the scenic coastline of the Sea of Marmara, Trilye is a charming fishing village in Bursa that captivates visitors with its blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Known for its well-preserved Greek architecture, narrow cobbled streets, and vibrant olive groves, Trilye offers a peaceful escape from city life. Stroll through colorful houses, visit historic landmarks like the Fatih Mosque (formerly a Byzantine church), and enjoy fresh seafood at seaside restaurants. Whether you're exploring its ancient churches or simply watching the sunset over the harbor, Trilye promises a serene and unforgettable experience.

Şehreküstü - Bazaars District

Located in the heart of Bursa, Şehreküstü is a vibrant and historical district that offers a true taste of the city’s spirit. Centered around the iconic Şehreküstü Square, it is home to the statue of Osman Gazi and surrounded by bustling streets, local shops, and cultural landmarks. The area is a perfect starting point for exploring nearby attractions such as the Grand Mosque (Ulu Camii), the Covered Bazaar (Kapalı Çarşı), and the historic Hanlar District. With its charming blend of Ottoman architecture, lively atmosphere, and easy access to public transport, Şehreküstü invites visitors to experience both the modern rhythm and rich heritage of Bursa

Gölyazı

Nestled on a slender peninsula jutting into Lake Uluabat about an hour west of Bursa, Gölyazı is a picturesque village steeped in history and natural charm. Once an ancient Greek city known as Apollonia ad Rhyndacum, its roots date back to the 6th century BC . Today, Gölyazı blends its Hellenistic and Ottoman heritage with stunning lakeside vistas, winding cobblestone streets, and traditional stone houses. Highlights include strolling along the tranquil lakefront, enjoying a boat tour to nearby islets with blooming water lilies, and dining at lakeside cafes serving freshly caught fish—particularly the famous local tuna—prepared over wood fire . Don’t miss the legendary “Weeping Plane Tree,” whose perennial stream of water is tied to a romantic local legend of star-crossed lovers
